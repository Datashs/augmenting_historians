# Confronting the Manuscript with the Corpus — Signal Examples

This document presents a selection of examples drawn from the automated confrontation between the manuscript and the corpus.
(scripts 06, 07)

⚠️ The elements below do not constitute a historical interpretation.
They present **signals produced by the pipeline**:
- alignments
- possible enrichments
- absence of correspondence

The suggested reformulations are **strictly indicative**: they make explicit what the outputs suggest, without guaranteeing their validity.

---

## 1. Alignment + definitional enrichment

**Manuscript excerpt**
> "L'extradition est une procédure technique. Fréquente, réglée par des centaines de traités bilatéraux…"

**Signal produced (possible corpus enrichment)**
- Precise legal definition available in the corpus
- Historical inscription (late 19th – early 20th century) narrows the dating

**Mobilisable excerpts (output)**
- "L'extradition est le mécanisme juridique par lequel un État… remet un individu…"
- "Il faudra attendre la fin du XIXe siècle pour qu'elle devienne un enjeu universel…"

**Observable effect**
→ The paragraph can be **densified by inserting a definition and temporal markers**

**Suggested reformulation (indicative)**
The text can integrate a stabilized legal definition and specify the moment of international generalization of extradition.

---

## 2. Thematic alignment + extension of actors

**Manuscript excerpt**
> "Ces histoires de l'extradition sont menées souvent en retraçant les étapes d'une affaire…"

**Signal produced (enrichment)**
- Addition of actors identified in the corpus: jurisdictions, diplomats
- Highlighting of doctrinal debates

**Mobilisable excerpts (output)**
- "Le XIXe siècle sera celui de la confrontation des points de vue…"
- "L'extradition politique concentre les enjeux de la répartition des compétences…"

**Observable effect**
→ Shift from a description centred on individual cases to a **pluralisation of analytical levels**

**Suggested reformulation (indicative)**
The text can explicitly integrate the diversity of actors and decision-making levels involved.

---

## 3. Chronological alignment + under-specification

**Manuscript excerpt**
> "Au cours du XIXe siècle… la fréquence augmente…"

**Signal produced (enrichment)**
- Precise datings available:
  - 1830s (development of treaties)
  - 1869 (extension to minor offences)

**Mobilisable excerpts (output)**
- "À partir de 1830, le droit extraditionnel se perfectionne…"
- "Il faut arriver à 1869… pour que les délits apparaissent…"

**Observable effect**
→ The passage can be **specified by dated milestones**

**Suggested reformulation (indicative)**
The text can replace a general trend with a dated sequence.

## 4. Confirmed reflexive signal + explicitation of biases

**Manuscript excerpt**
> "Ces instruments ont leurs limites… les biais qu'elles introduisent…"

**Signal produced (enrichment)**
- Explicitation of biases:
  - dependence on categorisation choices
  - decontextualisation of textual units

**Mobilisable excerpts (output)**
- "Les instruments… ne sont pas objectifs…"
- "Le traitement suppose de réduire les textes en unités comparables…"

**Observable effect**
→ The passage can be **strengthened by a technical explicitation of biases**

**Suggested reformulation (indicative)**
The text can specify the concrete mechanisms through which biases are produced.

## 5. Undocumented intuition (absence signal)

**Manuscript excerpt**
> "L'histoire de l'extradition… est rythmée par quelques grandes affaires…"

**Signal produced (absence)**
- No specific case proposed in the outputs
- Explicit suggestion to add documented examples from the corpus

**Observable effect**
→ The passage remains **empirically unsupported**

**Associated output**
- "Des exemples précis d'affaires… seraient nécessaires"

**Suggested reformulation (indicative)**
The text can be completed with identifiable empirical cases.

## 6. Absence of alignment detected

**Manuscript excerpt**
> "Il faut s'arrêter sur la façon dont cette procédure a existé dans l'espace public…"

**Signal produced (absence)**
- No directly mobilisable passage identified
- No aligned excerpt proposed

**Observable effect**
→ Area **not covered by the current corpus**

**Minimal authorised interpretation**
→ The passage is neither confirmed nor enriched by the available sources.
This is logically consistent here: the older legal treatises do not address the media reception of extradition cases.

## Summary of observed signals

- Outputs predominantly produce:
  - **complements (definitions, dates, quotations)**
  - **extensions (actors, levels of analysis)**
  - **absence alerts (missing examples)**

- Main effects are:
  - local densification of paragraphs
  - explicitation of implicit trends
  - identification of uncovered areas

- Outputs do not produce:
  - global structuring of the argument
  - hierarchisation of arguments
  - interpretive validation
